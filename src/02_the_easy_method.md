# The Easy Method

This book's objective is to move you into a new frame of mind. In the usual approach to quitting, you start out feeling like you're climbing a mountain, and spend the following weeks craving and feeling deprived. With this method, you start right away with a feeling of relief, as if you'd just been cleared of something that had been quietly weighing on you. From there, the further you go in life, the more you'll look back at this period and wonder how you ever spent so much of it on hentai in the first place. You'll look at other users with sympathy rather than envy, and without any smugness about it.

Provided you're not someone who never developed this habit (reading on behalf of a partner) or has already stopped (or is in the white-knuckle stretch of a "hentai diet"), it's essential to keep using normally until you've finished the book completely. This sounds contradictory, and the instruction to keep using draws more objection than anything else in the book, but as you read on, your desire to use hentai will reduce on its own. **Take this instruction seriously: trying to quit early will not help you.**

Many people never finish the book because they feel like they're being asked to give something up, and some deliberately ration themselves to a line a day just to put off the inevitable. Look at it this way: what have you actually got to lose? If you don't stop by the end of the book, you're no worse off than you are right now. It's a bet with nothing to lose and a real chance of a large gain.

Incidentally, if you haven't watched hentai in a few days or weeks but aren't sure whether you count as a user, an ex-user, or something in between, or if you notice your desire to watch is already fading as you read this material, then don't deliberately use hentai to masturbate just to "stay consistent" with the instructions. In that case, you're effectively already a non-user, we just need your brain to catch up with where your behaviour already is. By the end of the book, you'll be a contented non-user.

EasyPeasy is the opposite of the standard approach, which lists hentai's many downsides and says:
*"If I can just go long enough without it, eventually the craving will fade and I'll get my life back, free of this thing."*
That's the logical-sounding way to do it, and plenty of people attempt to quit this way every day. It's also very difficult to sustain, for a few specific reasons:

**Stopping any single session isn't the real problem.**
Every time you finish watching, you've technically "stopped" for that moment. On day one of a hentai-free streak you might feel powerfully convinced: *"I don't want this in my life anymore."* Every user who's ever tried to quit has felt that same conviction, just as strongly as you do. The actual problem is day two, or day ten, or session ten-thousand, when in one weak moment you tell yourself it's just one peek, want another straight after, and you're right back in it.

**Knowing the downsides just adds fear, which makes stopping harder, not easier.**
Tell someone their habit is quietly reshaping how they relate to intimacy or making real connection feel flat by comparison, and the instinctive response isn't to stop, it's to reach for something that offers quick relief from that exact discomfort, often the very thing they were just warned about.

**Every reason you have for wanting to stop can end up working against you.**
Two things happen. First, you start to feel like you're being made to give up a private comfort, a coping tool, an outlet, however you've come to see it. Second, those reasons create a kind of blind spot: we don't actually use hentai *because* of those downsides, we use it for some other pull entirely, and quitting "because of the downsides" never addresses that pull. The real question isn't why you should stop. It's why you want or feel you need to do it in the first place.

With EasyPeasy, we set aside, for now, all the reasons you'd like to quit. Instead, we look directly at the hentai habit itself and ask:

1. What is hentai actually doing for me?

2. Am I genuinely enjoying it, or just going through a motion?

3. Do I really need to keep doing something that quietly works against what I actually want?

The honest answer is that hentai does nothing for you. To be precise: it's not that the downsides of using it outweigh the upsides. It's that there are no upsides to begin with, none that the habit itself is actually responsible for.

Most users feel the need to justify their use somehow, but the justifications, "it helps me relax," "it's safer than real intimacy," "it's just fiction, so it's harmless," "I need the variety," are all fallacies that fall apart under examination.

We'll dismantle these one at a time. You'll come to see there's genuinely nothing here to give something up. More than that, there are real, tangible gains to being free of it: a steadier sense of well-being and ease around intimacy are just two. Once the illusion that life is somehow less enjoyable without hentai is cleared away, once you see that life isn't just as good without it but considerably better, and once any sense of missing out disappears, we'll circle back to all the genuine reasons to quit, and at that point they'll work *for* you instead of against you. They'll become positive support for the thing you actually want: a life lived free of dependence on hentai.
