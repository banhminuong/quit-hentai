It took a while to understand why. The answer is that the terror of withdrawal doesn't actually come from the withdrawal itself, which is mild. It comes from doubt and uncertainty. The liberating truth is that stopping hentai is genuinely easy. What makes it feel hard is indecision, and mourning something you've already correctly decided you don't want. Users can and do go for relatively long stretches without it at various points. The discomfort only arrives when you're wanting something you've decided you can't have. Remove the wanting, and there's nothing left to fight.

The key, then, is making the decision final and certain. Not hoping it will hold, but knowing it will. Never doubt or question it, quite the opposite: meet the thought with something like relief and even pleasure. If the decision is certain from the start, it's easy. Every person who's ever been caught in this trap would, if offered the genuine chance to go back to before it started, take that chance without hesitation. You have an equivalent choice today, in the other direction. Don't think of this as giving something up.

Once the final decision is made, you already are a non-user. A user is someone going through life dependent on this. A non-user is someone who isn't.
